Ode to a Module  /  September 2021



The man who builds with simple tools cries out: “Return to me the module, Architect, that we might understand each other! Let us build a common tongue, that I might see as you, and you might see as me.”

What need should a mason have for the art of drawing? The brick is his tool, his measure, his weapon. Lay down a wall with a single line and save the drawing from its own deceit, o module!

Carefully the cartographer measures his domain, that his map may be useful and trustworthy. How much more are you then, o module, who manifests territories from map alone? The Architect spurns this that is your true power.

Shame on us who misuse you, o module! You are made of measure, the unfolding of space itself. How cheap it is to refrain from your richness, to make you singular, to orphan you in perpetuity.

The authoritarian propagates a lone measure and proclaims victory for the module. His subjects lament! The grid stifles you, o module, in your collaboration. Your true success lies in multiplicity, locality, and openness.

With what ignorance, o module, does the Architect ever enlarge you? Do they not see the paralysis caused by their gluttony? Alone and encumbered, you have lost your agility; truly your fitness is not their concern.

The harmony of nature is the wisdom of the module; how blind we are to this! Do we not perceive the elements as they are, building blocks of all matter and life? Are not all things from many, one? Your proper and humble use, o module, shines forth from this substance of our being.

May the eyes of the Architect see such that the world is recreated in her mind. Let her coordinate the efforts of many men with this image—a shared consciousness. Your purpose, o module, is to structure this consciousness and give it form for just use. Will you unify our benevolent efforts into a solitary force?

Let us combine our myriad hands and bring revolution through your animating spirit; let us crave dwellings that are beautiful and good for all mankind; let us achieve this together, for disparate we cannot. O module, be our binding! Should this vision of prosperity not seduce all creators into anonymity?

A man who builds with simple tools, I plead to you, Architect. Cloak yourself in the ethos of the module and be wise.
